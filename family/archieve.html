Failed Project -

  • In my opinion, my family exists like any other family - existing like a puppet with society pulling the strings . Let me explain this. The roof over your head and the food that's on the table does not come free. To survive you need to be employed. - You've given up your freedom for survival. I've been doing that for sixty years - I was married, had a house, and two kids. To me the real shocker was I was married, but didn't have a wife, I had a house but no home, two kidsI but I didn't have a family. So I tried, with the little knowledge I had, to change that but failed.
  • The development of this project was meant to address this family issue. We generally meet at the restaurant, have a meal, have some small talk. This process is repeat. - in reality there is no real family communication.
  • So I thought creating a private family website would I'm open to express themselves so that you can better understand each other . I organized a cruise for the whole family to get together in a more relaxed manner. pictures of the cruise is still available. So this was the project that failed.
  • Why did it fail.
    • That may have misunderstood the message I was trying to put across.
    • They may have felt that I was forcing the family together and I that should leave things to sort out on their own.
    • I think the real problem was breakdown in communication - which I shall deal in greater detail later.

 

Life

  • Questions you need to ask yourself. Are you happy, free from stress and can afford the luxary of pursuing your passion or your dream? If you are, then I am happy that things turned out well for you.
  • Don't fret yourself if you are not - because probably more than 90% of the people around the world fall in this category. Although, slavery was abolished in the States a few centuries ago, strange to say, that in this modern advanced world, slavery still exists in one form or another.
  • Our lives are controlled by governments, corporations, employers, advertisements, social media and society in general. Sometimes we are paid pittance for the work you do for an employer as in sweatshops. In reality this tantamounts to blackmail.
  • This happens all over the world. Are you resigned to this sort of meaningless existence or are you going to do something about it?
  • Take charge of your life .
    • Before you try to improve yourself, I believe changes to the following will be crucial.
      • Be your own master - don't let anyone take that away from you, not even members from your own family.
      • Distinguish between want and need, do not let society dictate what you should wear or how you should behave.

 

Our family -

We have no debts, drug addiction or alcoholic dependence. We have some balance between your personal life and with relationship with each other as a family. So it is reasonable to conclude that we have done reasonably well. Could we have done better?
  • If we dare to be honest to ourselves, I believe we could have done better. How well do we really know each other. I think we tend to follow the principle of let sleeping dogs lie or don't rock the boat. This approach is taken when we meet with people are not members of the family. A family is special because they are the ones you would call first if you need help. So why are some of use closer to our friends than to our family? Shouldn't there be a special bond or trust between members of a family. Basically, are we prepared to share our feelings, hopes and dreams? Think about it.

My Concern -

  • My concern is that my family will decide to go their separate ways when I am no longer around. If this is going to be the end result, then any attempt to get the whole family closer together will be a waste of precious time. As parents we can only guide and support their development. Unforturnately, throughout my live I had no role model and faced a host of other problems. I had failed to guide them through the crucial period of their development. As a result, I could sense the distance between us. The only thing that kept me going was to make sure that you do not have to go through the same problems that I had. I never realize how important family bonding is until after I had retired.
  • When Jeff came back from the State, I bought a X'mas tree to start their social development. The idea was for each of us to invite a few friends so that we can get closer together. I was able to bring a few of my collegues, but there was absolutely no response from the rest of the family. This turned out to be an absolute failure and the tree was stored in the attic. I believe at that point of time we were not very close, in fact I think you had very poor impression of me as a father.
  • Why is this important? In the real world there are very few people you can trust. If you are at the top of the ladder you will have many friends, but should you fall from grace you would not find them around. There are people with glib tongues who can get you to do almost anything they want. Remember the number of people who are conned in real life or through the internet. How many people have been cheated by close friends or relatives. How many marriages have been broken or shattered through the wrong- doings of a partner. The lesson to be learnt here is that one must exercise good judgement when choosing your friends.
  • People you meet away from home will never reveal their true self. Especially at your place of work. If you have not moved around socially it would be difficult for you to distinguish the wheat from the chaff. Generally, you guests will be happy to join you. In such a relaxed atmosphere, be more relaxed and honest in expressing their thoughts. If they are your office friends, and if there is office politics, it is likely they will be on your side. Eventually, you will get the knack of judging character.
  • If I had left things alone, we would probably have two family meeings a year. That would not be sufficient to bring us closer togther. Hence the birthdays (from both of us), public holidays. Whether this brought us closer together or not, it's best you be the judge.

    • The absence of Ken and his family from Jeff's and Michaela's wedding was an eye-opener. We were a small group and the absence of about 50% of the guests was definately noticeable. No matter what the reason was, the fact is that there was a failure in communication.
    • Lydia and Michaela, my 2 daughters-in-law, have never made any attempt to get to know each other. I see no reason why the two of your cannot go out and be yourselves. I know there are differences because of your background and if you don't do anything you will forever be strangers within the family.
